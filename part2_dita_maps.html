Part II: DITA Maps

This module introduces DITA maps and their role in organizing and linking topics for publication.

Learning Objectives

Upon completing this module, you will be able to:

  • Define DITA maps and explain their primary function in organizing topics.
  • Describe how DITA maps enable hierarchical and conditional organization of content.
  • Understand the role of relationship tables in defining non-hierarchical links.
  • Explain how DITA maps influence navigation in various output formats.
  • What Are DITA Maps?
    DITA maps define the structure, sequence, and relationships of DITA topics, enabling flexible content organization and reuse.
  • Organization
    DITA maps offer advanced organizational capabilities including hierarchical and conditional structures and various collection types.
  • Relationship Tables (RelTables)
    Relationship tables in DITA maps define non-hierarchical links between topics, improving navigation in complex documentation.
  • Navigation
    DITA maps influence navigation in output formats, enabling automatic generation and context-sensitive structures.
